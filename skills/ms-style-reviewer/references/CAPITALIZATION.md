# Capitalization

## General rules

- Use sentence-style capitalization most of the time: capitalize the first word and proper nouns, lowercase everything else.
- Capitalize the first word of a sentence, heading, title, UI label (button name, checkbox label), or standalone phrase.
- Capitalize proper nouns (brand, product, and service names).
- Always capitalize the first word of a new sentence. Rewrite sentences that start with a word that's always lowercase.
- Do not use ALL UPPERCASE for emphasis.
  - ✓ Use italic sparingly for emphasis.
  - ✗ Use ALL CAPS for emphasis.
- Do not use all lowercase as a design choice.
- Do not use internal capitalization (e.g., *AutoScale*, *e-Book*) unless it is part of a brand name.
- Do not capitalize the spelled-out form of an acronym unless it is a proper noun.
  - ✓ United Nations (proper noun — capitalize)
  - ✗ Graphical User Interface (not a proper noun — do not capitalize)
- When words are joined by a slash, capitalize the word after the slash if the word before it is capitalized.
  - ✓ Country/Region
  - ✓ Turn on the On/Off toggle.
- For hyphenated compound words, see hyphen rules.

## Sentence-style capitalization in titles and headings

- Use sentence-style capitalization: capitalize the first word and lowercase the rest.
- Always capitalize proper nouns (brand, product, service names) in titles and headings.
- If a title or heading includes a colon, capitalize the first word after the colon.
- Apply sentence-style capitalization to blog posts, documentation articles, and press releases.

**Examples:**
- ✓ Watch your favorite HD movies, TV shows, and more
- ✓ 1 TB of cloud storage
- ✓ Choose the Office version that's right for you
- ✓ Can a search engine predict the World Cup winner?

## Title-style capitalization

Use title-style capitalization only where required: product/service names, blog names, book/song titles, article titles in citations, white paper titles, titles of people.

### Rules for title-style capitalization

- Always capitalize the first and last words.
  - ✓ A Home to Go Back To
- Do not capitalize *a*, *an*, or *the* unless it is the first word.
  - ✓ Microsoft on the Issues
  - ✓ The Official Microsoft Blog
- Do not capitalize prepositions of four or fewer letters (*on, to, in, up, down, of, for*) unless the preposition is the first or last word.
  - ✓ How to Personalize Windows
  - ✓ To Personalize Windows
  - ✓ Ryse: Son of Rome
  - ✓ Achieving Excellence in the Classroom Through Technology
- Do not capitalize *and, but, or, nor, yet, so* unless it is the first or last word.
  - ✓ Monitoring and Operating a Private or Hybrid Cloud
- Capitalize all other words: nouns, verbs (including *is* and forms of *be*), adverbs (including *very*, *too*), adjectives, and pronouns (including *this, that, its*).
  - ✓ Enterprise Agility Is Not an Oxymoron
  - ✓ This Is All There Is
  - ✓ Teaching Math Over and Over Again, in Less Time Than Before
- Capitalize the word after a hyphen if it would be capitalized without the hyphen or if it is the last word.
  - ✓ Self-Paced Training for Microsoft Visual Studio
  - ✓ Copy-and-Paste Support in Windows Apps
  - ✓ Five Essential Snap-Ins (last word)
  - ✓ Five Essential Snap-ins for Microsoft Management Console (not last word)
- Capitalize the first word of UI labels and API terms unless they are always lowercase (e.g., *fdisk*).
- In programming languages, follow the traditional capitalization of keywords and special terms.

## Reviewer checklist

- Flag ALL CAPS used for emphasis.
- Flag title-style capitalization where sentence-style is expected (headings, UI labels, article titles).
- Flag incorrectly lowercased proper nouns or product names.
- Flag internal capitalization that is not a brand name.
- Flag capitalized spelled-out acronyms that are not proper nouns.
- Flag missing capitalization after a colon in a title or heading.
- Flag inconsistent capitalization of words joined by a slash.
